What is a Monolith?


Typically…

  • Tightly coupled
  • Multi-responsibility/mixed concerns
  • Large deployable unit(s)
Monoliths are how we all built apps. It is often still how we start.

Challenges

Monolithic
Scaling
Scaling a monolith is often difficult. Size is a challenge, but the complexity of the codebase, inter-dependencies, networking, data sources, often make them very difficult to scale past a certain point.

Challenges

Monolithic
Release Cycles
The tight coupling and poor separation of concerns in most monoliths coupled with antiquated process lead to very long and complicated release cycles.

Challenges

Monolithic
Onboarding
Large, complex code bases are very hard for new developers to “wrap their heads around”.

Challenges

Monolithic
Feedback Loop
Long release cycles yield long feedback cycles. Long feedback cycles are the bain of customer responsiveness.

What if we could...

Develop quickly with fast feedback

Deploy quickly and confidently every time

The art of the possible by going Cloud Native.

What if we could...

Scale our teams and applications

Fail gracefully and recover quickly

The art of the possible by going Cloud Native.

Build the
Right Thing
Building the right thing for your customers and business to outpace and out innovate your competitors. The microservice movement is about maximizing the capability of your development teams.

Cloud Native
Confidence
Cloud native is about unlocking the potential of our organizations and the cloud by striving for high levels of confidence. Confidence allows us to build the right thing QUICKLY. However, it is not a free lunch.
